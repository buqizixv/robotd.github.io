Intuitive Surgical Unveils da Vinci Xi Successor with AI-Assisted Anatomy Recognition

Published May 12, 2026

Intuitive Surgical, the company that defined robotic surgery over the past two decades, has unveiled the successor to its da Vinci Xi platform at the 2026 Society of American Gastrointestinal and Endoscopic Surgeons (SAGES) annual meeting. The new system, temporarily designated the da Vinci XR (Extended Reality) pending FDA marketing clearance, represents the most significant update to the da Vinci line since the Xi launched in 2014. The headline feature is AI-Assisted Anatomy Recognition (A3R), a deep learning system that processes the surgical video feed in real time to identify and highlight critical anatomical structures — blood vessels, nerves, ureters, bile ducts — that surgeons must avoid or preserve. The system overlays semi-transparent color labels onto the surgeon's 3D console view, providing continuous visual guidance that looks similar to the augmented reality overlays in modern fighter jet helmets.

How A3R Works

A3R is built on a convolutional neural network trained on a curated dataset of over 500,000 annotated surgical video frames from more than 15,000 procedures across 200 hospitals worldwide. The data covers seven surgical specialties: urology, gynecology, colorectal, thoracic, head and neck, cardiac, and general surgery. The model runs on a dedicated GPU within the da Vinci vision tower and processes each video frame in under 30 milliseconds, ensuring no perceptible lag between the real surgical view and the AI overlay. In validation studies submitted to the FDA, A3R demonstrated 98.7% sensitivity for detecting ureters during colorectal surgery (one of the most common sites of inadvertent injury) and 96.2% sensitivity for detecting the recurrent laryngeal nerve during thyroid surgery. False positive rates were below 2% — important, because an overlay that incorrectly labels structures could be worse than no overlay at all. The system is designed to be probabilistic: structures that the model is highly confident about are highlighted boldly; ambiguous regions are either dimly highlighted or not shown, reflecting the model's uncertainty rather than making a potentially dangerous guess.

Beyond Anatomy: Skills Assessment and Training

A3R is not the only AI feature in the new platform. Intuitive has also introduced an automated skills assessment system that analyzes a surgeon's instrument movements during a procedure and provides objective metrics: economy of motion, idle time, off-screen time, and path efficiency. These metrics are benchmarked against a database of anonymized performance data from expert surgeons, allowing trainees and practicing surgeons to see precisely where their technique differs from the experts. The system does not record a 'score' — Intuitive is careful to avoid anything that could be used punitively — but rather provides a dashboard of metrics with comparative benchmarks. A study published alongside the platform launch showed that surgical residents who received weekly AI skills feedback achieved expert-level efficiency metrics in approximately 60% of the cases compared to those who received traditional instructor feedback alone. This has significant implications for surgical training, which has historically relied on an apprenticeship model with limited objective feedback.

Regulatory Path and Timeline

Intuitive has submitted the da Vinci XR with A3R to the FDA via the De Novo classification pathway, which is designed for novel medical devices with moderate risk that have no existing predicate device. The company expects FDA clearance in late 2026, with a controlled market release at 10-15 major US hospitals in early 2027, followed by broader commercial availability later that year. The system will be backward-compatible with existing da Vinci Xi instruments and accessories, protecting the investment of hospitals that have already built da Vinci programs. Pricing has not been announced, but analysts expect a 20-25% premium over the current da Vinci Xi system, which sells for approximately $1.8 million. Intuitive's installed base of approximately 8,500 da Vinci systems worldwide gives it a massive addressable upgrade market even before new customer acquisition.
